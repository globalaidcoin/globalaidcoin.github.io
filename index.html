Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Do you give Bitcoin to beneficiaries?+

Answer: No, we do not. In our view this would not make sense since in most countries only a tiny fraction of businesses will accept to be paid in Bitcoin. Instead we use custom built virtual currencies that behave just like fiat (real) currencies but are not exchangeable with any existing currency. For example, we can operate in the virtual equivalent of Pound(Lebanon), Lira(Turkey), Dinar(Jordan), Shilling(Uganda), Rupee(Pakistan), Naira(Nigeria) etc.

When do beneficiaries receive actual cash in local currency?+

Answer: Never. Our system works like an eCash system, but the beneficiaries cannot 'cash-out'. They can only receive goods/services that the participating merchant is prepared to hand out in return for the virtual currency credit he receives from the beneficiary.

I still don't understand. Are you providing eCash or eVouchers?+

Answer: The multitude of guidelines on Cash Based Interventions that are circulating in the aid community would probably categorize our system as 'Value eVouchers'. This means 1) The system is electronic in the sense that it does not use paper vouchers or physical cards. 2) The virtual currency amount we transfer to beneficiaries can be interpreted as a voucher that enables the beneficiary to purchase goods for the countervalue of the voucher. However, classical vouchers will have to be spent as a whole, while ours can be broken up and used in several transactions with different merchants and in that regard, the voucher behaves like a currency. So the term eCash would also be an appropriate description for what we do. You see that we do not exactly fit into any of the current categories. We are doing someting new!

Which mobile network operator do you use?+

Answer: We can work with any mobile network operator. We recommend to use the operator with the lowest price for sending text messages to local numbers.

Are you recording all transactions on the blockchain?+

Answer: Yes. Every single transaction is recorded on the blockchain, which essentially is a gigantic public ledger (we use Ethereum.org). But if the ledger is public, how can we protect the privacy of your beneficiaires? We would be happy to explain this to you in a phonecall or Skype call because it gets a bit technical. Send us an email and we will set one up.

How can you be exempt of "Know Your Customer" regulation?+

Answer: Please have a look at the "Monthly AidCoin Cycle" document. It shows that transactions between us, the beneficiaries and the merchants are not subject to KYC. The reason is simply that these virtual currency transactions are technically speaking "eVoucher" transactions. Since an eVoucher in and by itself cannot be exchanged into fiat (real) currency anywhere, it is of no concern to financial regulators. The reimbursement of the merchants by the Aid Program Coordinator however is subject to KYC. The privacy of beneficiaries remains protected.

How do the merchants/vendors get paid?+

Answer: Merchants/Vendors need to be paid by the aid program coordinator at least once a month based on the transaction reports that we generate and send to the aid program coordinator for this purpose. If required, the frequency of reports can be changed to weekly, but this would need to be justified by a high volume of transactions. The program coordinator can monitor transactions in realtime for any merchant and any beneficiary in the program, i.e. to estimate the reimbursement volume to expect at the end of the next month/week, or to detect and act on desired or undesired spending patterns.

Will the aid agency/NGO have to transfer all funds to globalAidCoin?+

Answer: No funds will have to be transferred to globalAidCoin. But the Aid Program Coordinator must be able to prove to us that sufficient funds are available for reimbursing the merchants at the end of the current monthly/weekly AidCoin cycle.

What happens when there is no mobile phone network?+

Answer: We rely on the GSM networks capacity to transmit short text messages between users. If this requirement is not met, you may want to consider using cash-in-envelopes, vouchers or other offline solutions.

Can you operate in country X and language Y using currency Z?+

Answer: Yes we can. The system is multi-language, multi-currency and we can work with any mobile network operator. We can even operate across national borders, but this is not advisable because it would require beneficiaries to send text messages to an international number in order to make a transaction.

What does it cost?+

Answer: There is a flat fee of 1.5% per transaction, charged to the aid program coordinator. The service is free for beneficiaries.
