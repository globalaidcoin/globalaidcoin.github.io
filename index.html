Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Do you give Bitcoin to beneficiaries?+

Answer: No, we do not. In our view this would not make sense since in most countries only a tiny fraction of businesses will accept to be paid in Bitcoin. We use custom built virtual currencies that behave just like fiat currencies (EUR,USD,GBP,etc.) but are not exchangeable with any existing currency.

When do beneficiaries receive actual cash in local currency?+

Answer: Short answer: Never. Long answer: Our system works like an eCash system, but the beneficiaries cannot 'cash-out'. They can only receive goods/services that the participating merchant is willing to hand out in return for the virtual currency credit he receives from the beneficiary.

Which mobile network operator do you use?+

Answer: We are not bound to any specific mobile network operator. We recommend to use the operator that has the lowest price for sending text messages to local numbers.

Are you recording all transactions on the blockchain?+

Answer: Yes. Every single transaction is recorded on the blockchain, which essentially is a gigantic public ledger. But if the ledger is public, how can we protect the privacy of your beneficiaires? We would be happy to explain this to you in a phonecall or Skype call. Send us an email and we will set one up.

How can you be exempt of "Know Your Customer" regulation?+

Answer: Please have a look at the "Monthly AidCoin Cycle" document. It illustrates why transactions between us, the beneficiaries and the merchants are not subject to KYC. The reimbursement of the merchants by the Aid Program Coordinator however is subject to KYC. The privacy of beneficiaries remains protected.

How do the merchants/vendors get paid?+

Answer: Merchants/Vendors are paid by the Aid Program Coordinator once a month. If required, this can be changed to once a week, but this would need to be justified by a high volume of transactions.

Will the aid agency/NGO have to transfer all funds to globalAidCoin?+

Answer: No funds will have to be transferred to globalAidCoin. But the Aid Program Coordinator must be able to prove to us that sufficient funds are available for reimbursing the merchants at the end of the current monthly/weekly AidCoin cycle.

What happens when there is no mobile phone network?+

Answer: Our operation relies on the GSM networks capacity to transmit short text messages between users. If this requirement is not met, you may want to consider using cash-in-envelopes, vouchers or other offline solutions.

Can you operate in country X and language Y using currency Z?+

Answer: Yes we can. The system is multi-language, multi-currency and we can work with any mobile network operator. We can even operate across national borders, but this is not advisable because it would require beneficiaries to send text messages to an international number in order to make a transaction.
